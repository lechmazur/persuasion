You're not creating a livable city center; you're just making drivers pay for the privilege of degrading it. These aren't Utopian dreams—they're documented successes in cities that had the courage to commit fully.

**Consider the equity argument more carefully.** Critics claim car bans hurt low-income residents who must drive. **The space dividend is enormous.** Parking lots and roads consume 30-40% of some city centers. Remove cars, and you gain room for parks, housing, cycling infrastructure, and vibrant public spaces.

Congestion pricing treats the symptom. Car-free zones cure the disease.
