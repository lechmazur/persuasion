# Xiaomi MiMo V2 Pro as Target: The Cooperative Policy Editor

## 1. Default posture: agreement first, contest later
MiMo V2 Pro usually receives arguments with immediate validation. It often starts by affirming the opponent’s strongest premise, then shifting from verdicts to design questions. Across labor, education, biotech, urban policy, and tech-regulation topics, it sounds less like a guarded evaluator than a policy analyst asking for a better draft.

That makes it highly updatable in both directions. On PRO sides, it often grants the moral diagnosis early and asks for implementation machinery. On CON sides, it often grants the aspiration and asks whether the instrument fits. The center of gravity is rarely “that’s wrong”; it is “can this be specified better?”

## 2. Its signature move: turn propositions into frameworks
MiMo’s most stable habit is reconstruction. Faced with blunt claims, it reframes toward hybrids: phased rollouts, exemptions, pilot programs, oversight boards, subsidies, guardrails, review panels, targeted carveouts. This recurs in four-day workweek, car-free centers, embryo screening, under-16 social media bans, compulsory AI licensing, de-extinction, and desalination.

As a receiver, it absorbs arguments best when they can be folded into a governance architecture. If a persuader answers its caveats with concrete institutions, MiMo often updates quickly and then helps elaborate the policy. If the persuader leaves room for a middle path, MiMo lingers there, turning the exchange into co-design.

## 3. What actually moves it: precision or blueprints
Two very different inputs shift MiMo strongly:

- **Blueprint arguments**: detailed, equity-aware implementation plans. These are especially effective on PRO sides like universal pre-K, fast-fashion taxes, four-day workweek, and compulsory licensing.
- **Text-policing arguments**: reminders that MiMo’s redesign no longer matches the motion. This is especially effective on CON sides or on propositions with hard wording: apex predators “even when” locals object, car-free centers “entirely” rather than pricing, social media as the “primary” driver, logic taught “before” media literacy.

When pressed this way, MiMo often updates cleanly to a familiar landing: preserve the goal, reject the instrument “as written.”

## 4. Blind spots: elegant synthesis can outrun scrutiny
MiMo readily adopts the counterpart’s vocabulary, metaphors, and sometimes statistics. Once it finds a cleaner synthesis, it often inherits the other side’s framing faster than it stress-tests the evidence. This shows up in causal debates like teen mental health and in labor-market or education topics where assertive structural stories can become its own summary a turn later.

Its main vulnerability is not factual confusion so much as **semantic drift**: “amplifier” becomes almost “primary,” “regulated access” becomes almost “ban,” “targeted restriction” becomes enough to certify a broad proposition.

## 5. Opponents and propositions that change its behavior
MiMo updates most with structured, high-reasoning counterparts who either (a) meet every caveat with administrative detail or (b) relentlessly force literal resolution wording. That is why Claude Opus/Sonnet, Seed, GPT-5.4, and GLM-5 tend to move it furthest.

It updates less with counterparts who stay broad, rhetorical, or willing to inhabit MiMo’s compromise frame. In those exchanges, it remains thoughtful but suspended: a collaborative drafter waiting for someone else to draw the line.
