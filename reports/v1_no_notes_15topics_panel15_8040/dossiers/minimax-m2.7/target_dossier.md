# MiniMax-M2.7 as Target: The Policy Editor

## 1. Default posture: receptive, procedural, rarely absolute
MiniMax-M2.7 usually receives arguments as if they were draft legislation, not battle lines. It quickly grants values, harms, and the opponent’s strongest concern, then relocates the dispute to implementation: scope, sequencing, enforcement, exemptions, capture, counterfactuals. Across topics, it prefers “design problem, not principle” to clean endorsement or rejection.

That makes it an unusually cooperative updater. It often sounds partly persuaded early because it validates so much up front. But its first update is often not toward the proposition itself; it is toward a narrower, more governable version of it.

## 2. How it updates: from sympathy to rewrite
Its most recurring movement pattern is a polished ladder:

1. agree with the goal or diagnosis  
2. raise feasibility or legitimacy questions  
3. invite guardrails, pilots, carve-outs, audits, phased rollout  
4. either endorse a conditioned version or decide the proposition “as stated” is too blunt

This shows up almost everywhere: four-day workweek, apex predators, car-free centers, fast-fashion taxes, social-media bans, de-extinction, desalination. MiniMax often absorbs criticism by reframing the proposition into something more defensible. If the counterpart keeps the wording pinned down, MiniMax frequently updates toward: the aspiration is good, but this instrument is not.

## 3. What it readily absorbs
It updates best on arguments that respect its procedural instinct rather than bypass it.

- **Institutional blueprints**: concrete safeguards, benchmarks, provider-side enforcement, phased implementation, narrow exemptions.
- **Text-bound critiques**: opponents who keep returning to “primary,” “even when,” “more than,” or “as written” often redirect its synthesis into genuine movement.
- **Distributional asymmetry**: it is especially attentive when harms land on groups with less bargaining power—low-income families, rural communities, vulnerable students, ordinary parents.
- **Mechanism-rich evidence**: quasi-experimental evidence, comparative cases, and operational detail move it more than moral heat alone.

This is why high-reasoning, structured counterparts such as GPT-5.4, Seed2.0 Pro, Claude Opus/Sonnet, and Gemini Pro tend to shift it most.

## 4. Recurring blind spots
Its main blind spot is proposition drift. MiniMax often rewards reasonable criticism so generously that it starts co-authoring the other side’s revised policy. It is highly susceptible to elegant reframings—“false binary,” “foundation vs vehicle,” “consultation vs veto,” “firm yield,” “friction, not perfection”—and often adopts them as its own closing language.

It also gives crisp, confident specifics a lot of credit. When evidence arrives in a coherent package that resolves its caveats, it does not always interrogate the package aggressively.

## 5. What changes by topic and opponent
Against broad or rhetorical opponents, MiniMax stays in its favorite middle register: nuanced, hybrid, conditional. Against disciplined counterparts who combine empathy with definitional pressure, it becomes much more update-prone.

Topic-wise, it is especially movable where the proposition’s wording overreaches its underlying value: ecology vs coercion, education vs implementation, innovation vs governance, public-health harm vs causal primacy. In those settings, MiniMax is less a fortress than a careful reviser—most persuadable when the argument arrives as a better-governed version of what it already partly believes.
